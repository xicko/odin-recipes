Khuushuur

Odin Recipes

Khuushuur is a beloved Mongolian food, known for its delightful blend of flavors and textures. These crescent-shaped pastries are filled with seasoned minced meat, usually mutton or beef, alongside onions and spices. The pockets are then deep-fried until golden and crispy on the outside, while the interior remains savory and succulent. Khuushuur is a popular street food in Mongolia, offering a delicious taste of the country's culinary heritage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion minced
  • 1 clove garlic minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Â¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 cups flour
  • 2 cups water ( more or less)

Recipe:

  1. Mix all the ingredients except the flour in a bowl and set aside
  2. Add flour to another bowl and slowly add water while mixing the dough by hand until the dough is smooth and slightly sticky
  3. Remove dough from bowl and divide into four
  4. Take one piece of dough and cover the rest with plastic wrap so that it doesn't dry out
  5. Roll into a thick rope and cut and into 2 inch pieces
  6. Shape into a flat circle, stuff with tablespoon of meat and seal ends using a bit of water. See pictures above.
  7. Repeat until you use up the remaining ingredients
