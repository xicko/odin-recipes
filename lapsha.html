Lapsha

Odin Recipes

Lapsha is a Mongolian noodle soup, enjoyed for its simplicity and heartiness. It consists of hand-pulled wheat noodles, meat (often mutton or beef), and assorted vegetables in a flavorful broth. Lapsha is a staple comfort food in Mongolia, known for its satisfying, warming qualities, and it's often prepared in various regional variations to suit local tastes.

Ingredients:

  • 500g of mutton or beef (include fat)
  • Oil
  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 turnip
  • 2 onions
  • 1 carrot
  • 2 tsp stock powder or salt (so about 4 cubes of beef stock)
  • 2-3 litres of water
  • 1 packet flat noodles (fresh or dried)

Recipe:

  1. Cut mutton or beef into strips.
  2. Dice turnip, onions and carrot.
  3. In a large wok, fry the sliced meat in oil.
  4. Add vegetables and stir fry these briefly.
  5. Add the water and stock and boil until almost cooked.
  6. Add noodles and continue boiling until these are ready.
  7. This soup should be able to serve more than 4 people, if you are looking to only feed 2, the meat and water can be halved to accommodate.
